## 1. What is a Bank

A bank is a financial institution that accepts deposits from the public, creates credit, and facilitates the flow of money through the economy. Banks are regulated by central banks and government authorities to ensure safety and soundness.

How it works: Banks take money from depositors (individuals and businesses) and use those funds to make loans to borrowers. They earn profit from the difference between the interest they pay on deposits and the interest they charge on loans. Banks also process payments, provide safekeeping, and offer various financial services.

## 2. Why Do Banks Exist

Banks exist to solve fundamental problems in the economy: the mismatch between savers and borrowers, the need for safe storage of money, and the necessity of a reliable payment system.

How it works: Without banks, savers would have to find borrowers directly (difficult and risky). Borrowers would struggle to find lenders. Money would be unsafe to store. Payments would be cumbersome. Banks solve these problems by acting as intermediaries: they pool deposits from many savers, evaluate borrowers, manage risk, and provide secure storage and efficient payment systems.

```
WHY BANKS EXIST

    PROBLEM 1: SAVERS AND BORROWERS DON'T MATCH
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Savers have excess money but no borrowers      |
    |  Borrowers need money but have no savers        |
    |  Banks connect them                             |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    PROBLEM 2: INDIVIDUAL LENDING IS RISKY
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Lending directly is risky                      |
    |  Banks pool risk across many borrowers          |
    |  Diversification reduces risk                   |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    PROBLEM 3: MONEY NEEDS SAFE STORAGE
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Cash under mattress is unsafe (theft, fire)    |
    |  Banks provide secure storage                   |
    |  Deposit insurance protects depositors          |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    PROBLEM 4: PAYMENTS ARE COMPLEX
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Paying people directly is difficult            |
    |  Banks provide checks, cards, wires, ACH        |
    |  Electronic payments are efficient              |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    PROBLEM 5: LONG-TERM VS SHORT-TERM
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Savers want short-term access                  |
    |  Borrowers want long-term funds                 |
    |  Banks transform short-term deposits into       |
    |  long-term loans (maturity transformation)      |
    +-------------------------------------------------+
```

The history of banking spans thousands of years, evolving from simple safekeeping to sophisticated financial systems.

```
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Medici family in Florence                      |

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    1694: Bank of England
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  First modern central bank                      |

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Created national banks                         |
    |  Standardized currency                          |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Created US central bank                        |
    |  Lender of last resort                          |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    |  Created FDIC (deposit insurance)               |

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    2008: Financial Crisis
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Lehman Brothers fails                          |

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
```

```
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    TYPE 7: COOPERATIVE BANKS
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Owned by members and customers                 |

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    TYPE 8: PRIVATE BANKS
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Serve wealthy individuals and families         |
```

## 9. Basic Banking Business Model

The banking business model is based on financial intermediation and the interest rate spread.

How it works: Banks attract deposits (liabilities) by offering interest and services. They use these funds to make loans and investments (assets). The difference between the interest earned on assets and the interest paid on liabilities is the net interest income. Banks also earn fees for services.

```
BASIC BANKING BUSINESS MODEL

                         +---------------------------+
                         |      CUSTOMERS            |
                         +-------------+-------------+
                                       |
          +----------------------------+----------------------------+
          │                            │                            │
          ▼                            ▼                            ▼
+---------------------------+  +---------------------------+  +---------------------------+
|  DEPOSITORS               |  |  BORROWERS                |  |  PAYMENT USERS            |
|  Provide funds            |  |  Use funds                |  |  Use services             |
|  Earn interest            |  |  Pay interest             |  |  Pay fees                 |
+---------------------------+  +---------------------------+  +---------------------------+
          │                            │                            │
          +----------------------------+----------------------------+
                                       │
                                       ▼
                         +---------------------------+
                         |          BANK             |
                         +-------------+-------------+
                                       |
          +----------------------------+----------------------------+
          │                            │                            │
          ▼                            ▼                            ▼
+---------------------------+  +---------------------------+  +---------------------------+
|  LIABILITIES (Deposits)   |  |  ASSETS (Loans)           |  |  INCOME (NII + Fees)      |
|  Pay interest to          |  |  Earn interest from       |  |  - Net Interest Income    |
|  depositors               |  |  borrowers                |  |  - Fee income             |
|  Cost of funds            |  |  Yield on assets          |  |  - Trading income         |
+---------------------------+  +---------------------------+  +---------------------------+

 Profit Formula:
 +-------------------------------------------------+
 |  Net Interest Income =                          |
 |  (Interest Earned) - (Interest Paid)            |
 |                                                 |
 |  Net Profit = Net Interest Income +             |
 |  Fee Income - Operating Expenses                |
 +-------------------------------------------------+
```

## 10. How Banks Make Money (Overview)

Banks generate revenue through two primary channels: net interest income and non-interest income.

How it works: Net interest income is the spread between what banks earn on loans and what they pay on deposits. Non-interest income comes from fees and services: overdraft fees, account maintenance, ATM fees, credit card interchange, wealth management fees, and trading revenue.

```
BANK REVENUE SOURCES

                         +---------------------------+
                         |     BANK REVENUE          |
                         +-------------+-------------+
                                       |
          +----------------------------+----------------------------+
          │                                                         │
          ▼                                                         ▼
+---------------------------+                            +---------------------------+
|  NET INTEREST INCOME      |                            |  NON-INTEREST INCOME      |
|  (60-70% of revenue)      |                            |  (30-40% of revenue)      |
+---------------------------+                            +---------------------------+
          │                                                            │
          ▼                                                            ▼
+---------------------------+              +---------------------------+---------------------------+
|  Interest Earned          |              |  Fees                    |  Other Services            |
|  - Loan interest          |              |  - Overdraft fees        |  - Wealth management       |
|  - Securities interest    |              |  - ATM fees              |  - Trading income          |
|  - Reserve interest       |              |  - Account maintenance   |  - Investment banking      |
|  - Fed funds interest     |              |  - Credit card fees      |  - Currency exchange       |
+---------------------------+              +---------------------------+---------------------------+
          │                                              │
          ▼                                              ▼
+---------------------------+              +---------------------------+
|  Interest Paid            |              |  Net Fee Income           |
|  - Deposit interest       |              |  - Fees earned            |
|  - Borrowing interest     |              |  - Less: costs            |
+---------------------------+              +---------------------------+

    Net Interest Income = Interest Earned - Interest Paid

    Efficiency Ratio = Non-Interest Expenses / Revenue
    Lower is better (50-60% is good)
```

## 16. Banks and Money Creation (Overview)

Banks create money through the lending process. This is one of their most important functions.

How it works: When a bank makes a loan, it credits the borrower's account with a new deposit. This creates new money that did not exist before. Through the money multiplier, an initial deposit can support multiple rounds of lending, expanding the money supply significantly. Central banks control this process through reserve requirements and monetary policy.

```
MONEY CREATION THROUGH BANKS

                         +---------------------------+
                         |    BANK MAKES LOAN        |
                         +-------------+-------------+
                                       |
          +----------------------------+--------------------------+
          │                                                       │
          ▼                                                       ▼
+---------------------------+                       +---------------------------+
|  NEW DEPOSIT CREATED      |                       |  NEW MONEY CREATED        |
|  Borrower account         |                       |  Money supply increases   |
|  credited with loan       |                       |  By full loan amount      |
|  amount                   |                       |                           |
+---------------------------+                       +---------------------------+
          │                                                       │
          +----------------------------+--------------------------+
                                       │
                                       ▼
                         +---------------------------+
                         |  MULTIPLIER EFFECT        |
                         +-------------+-------------+
                                       │
          +----------------------------+----------------------------+
          │                                                         │
          ▼                                                         ▼
+---------------------------+                            +---------------------------+
|  Deposit 1: $100,000      |                            |  Reserve: 10%             |
|  Loan 1: $90,000          |                            |  Total money created:     |
|  Deposit 2: $90,000       |                            |  $1,000,000               |
|  Loan 2: $81,000          |                            |  (10x multiplier)         |
|  Deposit 3: $81,000       |                            |                           |
|  ... continues            |                            |                           |
+---------------------------+                            +---------------------------+

    Money Multiplier = 1 / Reserve Requirement Ratio
    Money Supply = Monetary Base × Money Multiplier
```

## 19. Banks and Central Banks

The relationship between commercial banks and central banks is fundamental to the financial system.

How it works: Commercial banks hold reserve accounts at the central bank. They use these accounts for interbank settlement and to meet reserve requirements. The central bank sets monetary policy, which affects banks' lending rates and profitability. The central bank acts as lender of last resort, providing liquidity to banks during crises. The central bank regulates and supervises commercial banks.

```
    |  Result: High defaults, loan losses           |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    CAUSE 2: LIQUIDITY CRISIS
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Too much short-term funding                   |
    |  Depositors withdraw (bank run)                |
    |  Cannot access funding markets                 |
    |  Result: Forced asset sales, failure          |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    CAUSE 3: MARKET SHOCKS
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Interest rate rises                           |
    |  Asset price crashes                           |
    |  Currency devaluation                         |
    |  Result: Losses on positions, capital erosion  |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    CAUSE 4: POOR MANAGEMENT
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Inadequate risk management                   |
    |  Weak governance                             |
    |  Fraud and misconduct                        |
    |  Result: Regulatory penalties, loss of        |
    |  confidence                                  |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    CAUSE 5: REGULATORY FAILURE
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Inadequate supervision                      |
    |  Weak capital requirements                   |
    |  Insufficient oversight                     |
    |  Result: Problems go undetected, grow       |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    CAUSE 6: CYBER ATTACKS
    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Data breaches                                |
    |  Ransomware attacks                          |
    |  System disruptions                         |
    |  Result: Customer losses, reputation damage   |
    +-------------------------------------------------+
```

```
COMMON BANKING TERMS

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  ACH (Automated Clearing House)                |
    |  Electronic network for batch payments        |

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  AML (Anti-Money Laundering)                   |
    |  Laws and procedures to prevent money          |
    |  laundering                                    |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  APR (Annual Percentage Rate)                  |
    |  Total cost of borrowing per year             |
    |  Includes interest and fees                   |

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Basel III                                     |
    |  International banking regulatory framework   |
    |  Capital, liquidity, leverage standards       |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  CAMELS Rating                                 |
    |  Bank examination rating system               |
    |  Capital, Assets, Management, Earnings,       |
    |  Liquidity, Sensitivity                       |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1)                   |
    |  Highest quality bank capital                 |
    |  Common shares + retained earnings            |
    |  Minimum 4.5% of RWA                          |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  DTI (Debt-to-Income Ratio)                    |
    |  Total monthly debt payments / Monthly income |
    |  Used to assess borrower capacity             |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  Federal Funds Rate                            |
    |  Overnight lending rate between US banks      |
    |  Primary policy tool of Federal Reserve       |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  HQLA (High-Quality Liquid Assets)             |
    |  Assets easily convertible to cash            |
    |  Used for LCR                                 |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  IORB (Interest on Reserve Balances)           |
    |  Interest paid by central bank on reserves    |
    |  Used to implement monetary policy            |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  KYC (Know Your Customer)                      |
    |  Identity verification procedures             |
    |  Anti-money laundering requirement            |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio)                |
    |  HQLA / Net Cash Outflows (30 days) ≥ 100%   |
    |  Basel III liquidity standard                 |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  LTV (Loan-to-Value Ratio)                     |
    |  Loan Amount / Collateral Value               |
    |  Used for secured loans                       |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  NIM (Net Interest Margin)                     |
    |  (Interest Income - Interest Expense) /       |
    |  Earning Assets                               |
    |  Key profitability metric                     |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  NPL (Non-Performing Loan)                     |
    |  Loan with 90+ days past due                  |
    |  Problem asset for banks                      |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    |  Basel III stable funding standard            |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+
    |  OMO (Open Market Operations)                  |
    |  Central bank buying/selling securities       |
    |  Affects reserves and interest rates          |
    +-------------------------------------------------+

    +-------------------------------------------------+

    |  Collateralized short-term borrowing          |
    |  Sell securities, repurchase later            |
```
